Grilled Cheese Sandwich

A grilled cheese sandwich is a classic comfort food made by grilling cheese between two slices of bread until the bread is crispy and golden and the cheese is melted and gooey.

Ingredients

  • 1/4 cup unsalted butter, softened, divided
  • 8 (1/2-inch-thick) sourdough bread slices
  • 8 ounces extra-sharp white cheddar cheese, shredded (about 2 cups), divided
  • 4 ounces Gruyère cheese, shredded (about 1 cup), divided
  • Cooking spray
  • 2 2/3 cups Parmesan cheese, finely shredded (about 6 ounces), divided

Instructions

  1. Preheat oven to 250°F. Place a wire rack inside a large rimmed baking sheet; set aside.
  2. Coat a large nonstick skillet with cooking spray, and heat over medium. Sprinkle 1/3 cup Parmesan cheese in skillet in a shape slightly larger than bread slice.
  3. Place 1 sandwich in skillet on top of cheese mound, and cook until cheese in sandwich is starting to melt and Parmesan is lightly browned, 2 to 4 minutes
  4. Transfer sandwich to prepared baking sheet, Parmesan side down. Sprinkle another 1/3 cup Parmesan in skillet in a shape slightly larger than bread slice.
  5. Place cooked sandwich, buttered side down, on top of Parmesan; cook until cheese in sandwich is melted and Parmesan is lightly browned, 2 to 3 minutes.
  6. Transfer sandwich to prepared baking sheet; place in oven, and keep warm.
  7. Repeat procedure with remaining 3 sandwiches and remaining 2 cups Parmesan.
  8. Serve hot.
